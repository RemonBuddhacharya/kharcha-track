Meet the Cast

Every app has characters. In Kharcha Track, the six database models are like actors in a theater company — each with a specific role to play.

👤
User — The Person

Has roles (admin or regular user), email, and password. Everything in the app belongs to a user — they are the starting point of all data.

⭐
Expense — The Star

Title, amount, date, category, payment method. This is the main thing being tracked — the lead actor that every other character supports.

📁
Category — The Organizer

Groups expenses into buckets like Food, Transport, and Bills. Each user has their own categories plus shared system categories.

📜
ExpenseHistory — The Historian

Automatically records every create and update as a timeline entry. You never call it directly — it watches silently in the background.

🔮
Forecast — The Oracle

Uses past spending to predict future months. Stores predictions with confidence scores so you know how reliable each guess is.

🔍
Anomaly — The Detective

Scans expenses for unusual amounts and flags them with scores. If something looks off compared to your normal spending, this model catches it.

How They're Connected

These six actors don't work alone. They're linked together through relationships — here's who talks to whom.

Relationships
👤 User (1) ──▶ (∞) ⭐ Expense
👤 User (1) ──▶ (∞) 📁 Category
📁 Category (1) ──▶ (∞) ⭐ Expense
⭐ Expense (1) ──▶ (∞) 📜 ExpenseHistory
👤 User (1) ──▶ (∞) 🔮 Forecast
👤 User (1) ──▶ (∞) 🔍 Anomaly
Each arrow reads: "one ___ has many ___." A User has many Expenses. A Category has many Expenses. An Expense has many History entries.
💡
Key Pattern

This pattern — where one thing "has many" of another — is called a one-to-many relationship. It's one of the most common patterns in software. Almost every app you use has this at its core.

Code Translation: The Expense Model

Here's a real piece of code from Kharcha Track. On the left, what the developer wrote. On the right, what it actually means.

app/Models/Expense.php 
protected $fillable = [
    'user_id',
    'title',
    'description',
    'amount',
    'date',
    'category_id',
    'payment_method',
    'is_recurring',
    'is_anomaly',
];
Plain English
"Here are the fields that an expense can have..."
user_id — Who owns this expense
title — What you spent on
description — Optional details about the purchase
amount — How much it cost
date — When it happened
category_id — Which category it belongs to
payment_method — How you paid (cash, card, UPI...)
is_recurring — Does it repeat monthly?
is_anomaly — Was it flagged as unusual?

The Stage: Livewire Components

The models are the actors, but they need a stage. Livewire components are the pages where users actually interact with the data.

📋
expenses/index

The expense list with search, sort, add, edit, and delete. This is where users spend most of their time.

🔮
expenses/forecasting

The crystal ball page. Shows predicted spending for future months based on your history.

🔍
expenses/anomalies

The detective page. Lists flagged expenses that look unusual compared to your normal spending.

📁
categories/index

Manage your spending categories. Create, rename, or delete the buckets that organize your expenses.

📈
dashboard

Your personal spending overview with charts, totals, and trends at a glance.

🔒
admin/users/index

(Admin only) Manage all users in the system. Regular users never see this page.
